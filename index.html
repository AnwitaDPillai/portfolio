PROJECTS

Gold as Grammar

100,000 Words  |  Draft Complete

Currently authoring a 100,000-word monograph examining how women in colonial South Asia used material objects—gold, salt, cloth—as grammars of political refusal, 1900–1950. The book-length investigation analyses the physical, tactile objects and underground logistical infrastructures that structured anti-colonial resistance across South Asia.
